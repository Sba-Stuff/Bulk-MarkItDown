Introduction and General Overview

# What is Cyber Security?
Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks
These attacks often aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information
Cyber security helps ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

<!-- Slide number: 3 -->
# Why is Cyber Security Important?
Protects personal and organizational data
Prevents financial loss and fraud
Maintains trust with customers and partners
Ensures business continuity

<!-- Slide number: 4 -->
# Common Cyber Threats
Phishing and social engineering attacks
Malware (viruses, ransomware, spyware)
Password attacks
Unsecured Wi-Fi and devices

<!-- Slide number: 5 -->
# Phishing Attacks
Fake emails or messages that appear to be from trusted sources
Designed to steal passwords, personal data, or money
Often create urgency or fear
Always verify links and senders before clicking

<!-- Slide number: 6 -->
# Malware
Malicious software designed to harm or exploit devices
Includes viruses, ransomware, and spyware
Can spread through email attachments, downloads, or USB devices
Keep systems and antivirus software updated

<!-- Slide number: 7 -->
# Password Security Best Practices
Use strong, unique passwords
Avoid sharing passwords
Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)
Do not reuse work passwords on personal sites

<!-- Slide number: 8 -->
# Safe Internet and Email Usage
Do not click suspicious links or attachments
Be cautious when sharing personal or company information
Log out of systems when not in use
Report suspicious activity immediately

<!-- Slide number: 9 -->
# Your Role in Cyber Security
Cyber security is everyone’s responsibility
Human error is a major cause of security incidents
Following policies and best practices reduces risk
When in doubt, ask or report

<!-- Slide number: 10 -->
# Key Takeaways
Cyber threats are real and constantly evolving
Awareness is the first line of defense
Think before you click.
Protecting information protects everyone.
